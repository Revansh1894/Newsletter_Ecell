Week's Prime

Indian Startup Funding to Moon in H1 2021

Resources

It has only been six months into the year, and the funding levels have already exceeded those in the entire previous year. The pandemic has significantly increased the pace of digitalization in the country, which can be seen in the funding patterns.

Indian startups have raised $12.1 billion from venture capitalists and private equity firms until June, about $1 billion more than the total funding in 2020. What's more interesting is that the early-stage startups have seen a considerable amount of capital infusion. Also, there were 31 mega funding rounds recorded till June 30, which is the highest to date. In these rounds, the late-stage startups attracted significant funding from the investor ecosystem.

Halfway into the year, we have seen a unicorn boom in the country with the addition of 16 new unicorns. In addition to fintech, other sectors like health tech and social commerce also saw new unicorn additions. Innovaccer emerged as the first health tech startup and Pharmeasy( received funding of $350M) the first pharmacy to achieve unicorn status.

Fintech (about $1.89 billion across 98 deals) and Edtech ($1.41 billion across 65 deals) sectors lead the funding, closely followed by the food-tech sector(with only two deals from the two majors Zomato and Swiggy).

According to experts, the funding will touch record levels this year, with the startups raising anywhere between $14B to $17B.

Crisps

Bhutan now adopts India's BHIM-UPI payments system

After the resounding success of the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) in India, NPCI will now launch BHIM-UPI in Bhutan in collaboration with the Royal Monetary Authority of Bhutan. The launch will benefit more than 2,00,000 tourists from India who travel to Bhutan each year, as well as the Bhutanese people.

Digital payments startup MobiKwik files for Rs 1,900 Cr IPO

Mobikwik’s IPO could hit the markets by September. Shareholders who could put their stakes up for sale in the startup include American Express, Bajaj Finance, Cisco Systems, Sequoia Capital India, and Tree Line Asia Master Fund, among others.

Phani Kishan gets promoted to Co-Founder at Swiggy

This comes just weeks after Swiggy’s chief operating officer, Vivek Sunder called it quits, Phani Kishan Addepalli has been one of the early employees of the Bangalore-based food tech company, joining the team in March 2015. Addepalli started as general manager, and currently serves as vice president of strategy and investments in Swiggy.

Paytm officially files DRHP for IPO, to raise Rs 16,600 Cr

Paytm hopes its initial public offering to help it raise Rs 16,600 crore ($2.2 billion) in net proceeds. At the price tag of $2.2 billion, Paytm's will be the largest stock market listing ever in the country in over a decade, beating the IPOs of Coal India ($2.06 billion), Reliance Power ($1.61 billion), General Insurance Corporation (GIC) ($1.55 billion), and Oil and Natural Gas Corp (ONGC) ($1.45 billion).

Funding

Flipkart has raised another round from GIC, CPP Investments, SoftBank Vision Fund 2, and Walmart of USD 3.6 billion. Flipkart is valued at USD 37.6 billion as a result of the investment.

Trell, a platform for social commerce in the lifestyle, has raised $45 million in a Series B round of fundraising headed by banking firm Mirae Asset and H&M Group, with LB Investments on board as a co-lead. This round included existing investors such as KTB Network, Samsung Ventures, and Fosun RZ Capital.

Oliveboard, a Bengaluru-based Edtech platform, has raised INR 23 crore in a Pre-Series A round of funding. Education Catalyst Fund (ECF) and Yukti Securities also joined in the investment round, which was led by IAN Fund.

Bikry, an all-in-one platform for doing business online, has raised $1.3 million in the funding round headed by YCombinator and including Twitch.TV, RazorPay, Angelist, Cabra VC, and Sumon Sadhu.

Following the rise of Inshorts' Public app, a location-based social network that links people in their area, the company has raised $60 million. Its backers included Addition, Tiger Global, SIG, A91, and Tanglin Venture Partners.
